The Naga Balimbur motif on Sasirangan fabric depicts a dragon—a legendary creature in Banjar mythology—swimming or bathing in vast, deep waters in the morning with great joy. The name “Balimbur” comes from the word limbur or berlimbur, which means to wash or bathe, and philosophically symbolizes the process of self-purification and returning to one's true nature through joyfully facing life. In addition, this motif is also historically identified with warriors and palace officials—emphasizing the social status and courage of the wearer. The meanings contained therein include spiritual purity, fighting spirit, strength, as well as joy and optimism in responding to the dynamics of life. In summary, the Naga Balimbur motif is a symbol of spiritual and moral strength: it symbolizes the journey of a person who is pure, courageous, joyful, and able to face challenges with a noble spirit—in line with the meaning that all of this begins with cleansing oneself to achieve purity and love of life.
