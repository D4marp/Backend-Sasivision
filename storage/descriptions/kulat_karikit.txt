The Kulat Karikit motif imitates the shape of kulat / mushrooms that commonly grow attached to tree trunks or branches; visually, this motif often appears as a series of repeated dots, curves or small branches, giving it an organic and growing impression. Its philosophy is tied to symbols of nature: this motif symbolizes life, growth, and connection to nature/the forest — it is also sometimes understood as a sign of familiarity and fertility because mushrooms appear in fertile environments. Due to its natural elements and “attached/growing” form, this motif is often associated with the meanings of preservation, sustainability, and the relationship between humans and the environment in the Banjar tradition.
