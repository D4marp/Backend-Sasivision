The Bintang Bahambur motif in Sasirangan fabric is a visual representation of stars scattered across the sky, symbolizing how the universe is full of small elements that radiate beauty in harmony. The philosophy behind it builds hope that human life should be peaceful, simple, and always uphold noble ideals. The origins of Sasirangan fabric itself come from the Banjar tradition—a resist dyeing technique using a needle—which has been passed down from generation to generation since the 12th to 14th centuries. Folklore says that the fabric was first made to fulfill the request of Princess Junjung Buih, as a symbolic representation of the local wisdom and spirituality of the Banjar people.
